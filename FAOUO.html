FAQ's

What is the difference between need-based and merit-based financial aid?

Need-based financial aid is determined by your demonstrated financial need, considering factors such as family income and circumstances. Merit-based aid is awarded based on academic achievements, leadership, or other accomplishments, irrespective of financial need.

How will I know if I am eligible for a particular scholarship?

Eligibility criteria for each scholarship are outlined in the "Available Scholarships" section. Criteria may include minimum GPA requirements, enrollment in specific programs, financial need, community service involvement, leadership qualities, and more. Review the scholarship details to determine your eligibility.

When is the deadline for scholarship applications?

The application deadlines vary for each scholarship. Please refer to the specific scholarship details in the "Available Scholarships" section for information on application deadlines. Be sure to submit your application before the specified deadline to be considered.

How can I contact the Financial Aid Office for further assistance?

Feel free to reach out to us by filling out the contact form in the "Contact Us" section on our website. Provide your name, email, contact number, and a brief message outlining your inquiry. We'll get back to you as soon as possible.

How will I be notified if I am awarded a scholarship?

Notification processes may vary, and details are typically provided in the scholarship description. Some scholarships may notify recipients via email, while others may require additional steps. Keep an eye on your email and regularly check the status of your application on our website.
